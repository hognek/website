Poetry

Words that point to what cannot be named.

Where Love Breathes

Your eyes find mine, across silence.
Something nameless, stirs,
Not memory, not desire,
But recognition.

Fingertips trace invisible maps,
Each touch unveiling landscapes,
Neither familiar, nor strange.
As if returning to source.

The candle, flickers between us,
One flame, two shadows merging,
Dancing along walls that seem
Less solid than before.

We name things, to know them together,
This warmth, this current,
This dissolving, at the edges
As the veils of separation slowly thin.

What remains, when names fade?
When stories sleep?
When the night holds nothing
But this moment, endlessly opening?

Our whispers blend softly,
Knowing beyond thought,
In a tender, shared silence,
Love breathes, unspoken.

Two Hearts, One Beat

Your heartbeat against my chest,
a quiet, wordless whisper.

•

At first, a conversation.
Yours quick and soft with life,
mine slow and grounded,
learning to move as one.

•

The space between beats
begins to thin.

Your pulse becomes mine,
mine becomes yours.
There never was
a difference.

•

I cannot tell anymore
which heart leads,
which follows.

Only that this rhythm
has always been playing,
before we lay down together,
before we were born,
before we were we.

•

One beat,
two chests,
two breaths,
two names,
one whisper.

•

Beneath the illusion
of separation,
one Heart beats
through all things,

the same pulse
that moves oceans,
turns seasons,
and draws lovers
to lie this close.

•

So we rest here,
breathing each other's breath,
two waves
remembering
we were always
the same ocean.
